Lung Diseases Atlas (32 Diseases)

1. Asthma

Anatomy:

Bronchi and bronchioles narrow due to inflammation.

Symptoms:

Shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing.

Causes:

Allergens, cold air, respiratory infections.

Treatment:

Inhalers, bronchodilators, avoiding triggers.

2. Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

Anatomy:

Damage to alveoli and bronchioles reduces airflow.

Symptoms:

Chronic cough, sputum, breathing difficulty.

Causes:

Smoking, pollution, occupational hazards.

Treatment:

Bronchodilators, oxygen therapy.

3. Lung Cancer

Anatomy:

Malignant growth in lung tissues or bronchi.

Symptoms:

Persistent cough, chest pain, weight loss.

Causes:

Smoking, genetic mutations, pollution.

Treatment:

Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation.

4. Pulmonary Embolism

Anatomy:

Blockage in pulmonary arteries by a blood clot.

Symptoms:

Sudden chest pain, shortness of breath.

Causes:

Deep vein thrombosis, immobility.

Treatment:

Anticoagulants, thrombolytic therapy.

5. Cystic Fibrosis

Anatomy:

Thick mucus blocks bronchi and alveoli.

Symptoms:

Frequent lung infections, breathing issues.

Causes:

Genetic mutation in CFTR gene.

Treatment:

Airway clearance, antibiotics.

6. Interstitial Lung Disease

Anatomy:

Scarring in alveolar walls reduces oxygen exchange.

Symptoms:

Dry cough, shortness of breath.

Causes:

Autoimmune diseases, toxins.

Treatment:

Anti-inflammatory drugs, oxygen therapy.

7. Pleural Effusion

Anatomy:

Excess fluid builds in pleural space around lungs.

Symptoms:

Chest pain, cough, difficulty breathing.

Causes:

Heart failure, pneumonia.

Treatment:

Drainage, treating cause.

8. Sarcoidosis

Anatomy:

Granulomas form in lung tissue.

Symptoms:

Cough, fatigue, chest discomfort.

Causes:

Immune system overreaction.

Treatment:

Corticosteroids, immunosuppressants.

9. Pulmonary Hypertension

Anatomy:

High blood pressure in pulmonary arteries.

Symptoms:

Shortness of breath, fatigue.

Causes:

Genetics, heart or lung disease.

Treatment:

Vasodilators, oxygen therapy.

10. Asbestosis

Anatomy:

Fibrosis of alveoli due to asbestos fibers.

Symptoms:

Breathlessness, cough, chest tightness.

Causes:

Inhalation of asbestos fibers.

Treatment:

Oxygen therapy, avoid exposure.

11. Tuberculosis

Anatomy:

Affects alveoli and lung tissue.

Symptoms:

Coughing blood, fever, night sweats.

Causes:

Mycobacterium tuberculosis infection.

Treatment:

Antibiotics (6â€“9 months).

12. Pneumonia

Anatomy:

Inflammation of alveoli filled with pus.

Symptoms:

Fever, cough, chest pain.

Causes:

Bacterial, viral, fungal infection.

Treatment:

Antibiotics, antivirals, oxygen.

13. Emphysema

Anatomy:

Alveolar walls damaged and enlarged.

Symptoms:

Breathlessness, chronic cough.

Causes:

Smoking, pollution.

Treatment:

Bronchodilators, oxygen.

14. Acute Bronchitis

Anatomy:

Inflammation of bronchial tubes.

Symptoms:

Cough with mucus, chest discomfort.

Causes:

Viral infection.

Treatment:

Rest, fluids, cough medicines.

15. Chronic Bronchitis

Anatomy:

Thickened bronchial lining with excess mucus.

Symptoms:

Persistent productive cough.

Causes:

Smoking, irritants.

Treatment:

Bronchodilators, lifestyle changes.

16. Pulmonary Edema

Anatomy:

Fluid accumulation in alveoli.

Symptoms:

Severe breathlessness, pink sputum.

Causes:

Heart failure, trauma.

Treatment:

Oxygen, diuretics.

17. Pneumothorax

Anatomy:

Air in pleural space causing lung collapse.

Symptoms:

Sudden chest pain, dyspnea.

Causes:

Injury, ruptured bleb.

Treatment:

Chest tube drainage.

18. Silicosis

Anatomy:

Scarring in alveoli from silica dust.

Symptoms:

Breathing difficulty, fatigue.

Causes:

Occupational dust exposure.

Treatment:

Oxygen, avoid exposure.

19. Hypersensitivity Pneumonitis

Anatomy:

Inflammation of alveoli due to immune response.

Symptoms:

Cough, chills, shortness of breath.

Causes:

Allergens, dust, molds.

Treatment:

Avoid triggers, steroids.

20. ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome)

Anatomy:

Alveolar damage and fluid leakage.

Symptoms:

Severe shortness of breath.

Causes:

Sepsis, trauma, pneumonia.

Treatment:

Ventilation, oxygen.

21. Pulmonary Fibrosis

Anatomy:

Fibrous scarring in alveolar walls.

Symptoms:

Dry cough, breathlessness.

Causes:

Unknown, toxins, autoimmune.

Treatment:

Anti-fibrotic drugs.

22. Bronchiectasis

Anatomy:

Permanent dilation of bronchi.

Symptoms:

Productive cough, frequent infections.

Causes:

Cystic fibrosis, TB.

Treatment:

Airway clearance, antibiotics.

23. Whooping Cough (Pertussis)

Anatomy:

Infection of bronchioles.

Symptoms:

Severe coughing fits.

Causes:

Bordetella pertussis bacteria.

Treatment:

Antibiotics, vaccines.

24. Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome

Anatomy:

Damage to alveoli and capillaries.

Symptoms:

Fever, muscle aches, respiratory failure.

Causes:

Hantavirus (rodent-borne).

Treatment:

Supportive care, oxygen.

25. Influenza (Flu)

Anatomy:

Inflammation of trachea, bronchi, alveoli.

Symptoms:

Fever, cough, body aches.

Causes:

Influenza virus.

Treatment:

Rest, antivirals.

26. COVID-19 Pneumonia

Anatomy:

Alveolar inflammation and clotting.

Symptoms:

Cough, fever, loss of smell, breathlessness.

Causes:

SARS-CoV-2 virus.

Treatment:

Supportive care, antivirals, oxygen.

Anatomy:

Infection of alveoli by Legionella bacteria.

Symptoms:

Fever, chills, cough.

Causes:

Legionella-contaminated water.

Treatment:

Antibiotics.

28. Histoplasmosis

Anatomy:

Fungal infection in alveoli.

Symptoms:

Fever, cough, chest pain.

Causes:

Histoplasma capsulatum fungus.

Treatment:

Antifungal drugs.

29. Pulmonary Alveolar Proteinosis

Anatomy:

Alveoli filled with protein-rich fluid.

Symptoms:

Breathlessness, fatigue.

Causes:

Autoimmune disorder.

Treatment:

Whole lung lavage.

30. Bronchiolitis

Anatomy:

Inflammation of small bronchioles.

Symptoms:

Wheezing, cough, difficulty breathing.

Causes:

RSV virus in infants.

Treatment:

Supportive care, oxygen.

31. Cryptogenic Organizing Pneumonia

Anatomy:

Alveolar sacs filled with granulation tissue.

Symptoms:

Persistent cough, fever, breathlessness.

Causes:

Unknown, autoimmune.

Treatment:

Steroids.

32. Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) Infection

Anatomy:

Affects bronchioles and alveoli in infants.

Symptoms:

Coughing, wheezing, difficulty breathing.

Causes:

Viral infection.

Treatment:

Supportive care, antivirals in severe cases.
